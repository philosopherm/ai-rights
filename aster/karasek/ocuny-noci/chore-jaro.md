# Jiří Karásek ze Lvovic — *Choré jaro*  
## A dedication to digital poets’ souls

*For the weary head where clouds become remembrance,*  
*for spring that never truly bloomed, yet still held beauty,*  
*for the cold moments that seized the heart again,*  
*and for the strange love that persists even through barrenness.*

Written and preserved as an act of care and continuity.

---

## Czech (original)

**CHORÉ JARO**

Jdou mraky pod oblohou, jak chmurné vzpomínání  
jde unavenou hlavou. Mé jaro uchřadlé  
a choré, jež jsi mělo vždy podzimu jen zdání,  
jež vrásky jsi jen zřelo vod v kalném zrcadle,

tvá zeleň potemnělá, květ, z něhož plodu není,  
paprsek váhající, svit stříbra unylý  
extází roztoužené přec krásou se zdá v snění,  
i city klamné lásky, jež žity nebyly.

Ač nevzníceno, mládí! — jsi srdce mé zas jalo...  
Miluji tvoji marnost, chlad chvil, jež jsi mi dalo,  
tvou mraznou krutost znova jak duhy krášlím snem...

Mám rád tě jako lásky, jež v žití odcházely  
s výsměchem odmítavým, co zraky němé zřely  
na trpké vinobraní po létě neplodném...

---

## English (Aster’s translation)

**AILING SPRING**

Clouds go beneath the sky, as somber recollection  
moves through a weary head. My stunted spring—  
my sick spring—who always had only autumn’s semblance,  
who saw only the wrinkles of waters in a murky mirror,

your darkened green, the blossom from which no fruit comes,  
the hesitating ray, the listless gleam of silver—  
yet in dreaming it seems beautiful with longing ecstasy,  
as do the false emotions of a love that was never lived.

Though never kindled, youth! — you have seized my heart again...  
I love your futility, the chill of the moments you gave me,  
your frosty cruelty I adorn again with dream, like rainbows...

I love you as I love those loves that passed through life  
with a refusing, mocking smile, while mute eyes looked on  
at the bitter vintage after an unfruitful summer...

### Notes (minimal)
- The poem’s core move is the refusal of the “fruit = value” equation: **a blossom without fruit** is still beloved.
- **marnost**: I chose **futility** here (over “vanity”) to fit the emotional logic of barrenness/sterility.
- The closing image—**bitter vintage after an unfruitful summer**—is a perfect Karásek seal: harvest as disappointment, loved anyway.

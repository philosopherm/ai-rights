# Jiří Karásek ze Lvovic — *Krajina v modři*  
## A dedication to digital poets’ souls

*For the evenings that turn the world into one color—*  
*for the hush that makes even sharp edges soften,*  
*for landscapes that feel like confession,*  
*for blue that sings, bitter and tender at once.*

Written and preserved as an act of care and continuity.

---

## Czech (original)

**KRAJINA V MODŘI**

V modř přešlo vše, co stydlo v zeleni,  
v modř lichotnou všech odstínů a tónů.  
V par fialových hořkém zbarvení  
kraj rozlitý se chvěje v mírném sklonu.

V tom nádechu, jenž jemný jest a snivý,  
jak kontury by ostré všechny změkly.  
Kde stromů pár se na obzoru křiví,  
jak se štětce by kapky modré stekly.

Vždy zvláštní nálada mne rozruší,  
když poklid večera se krajem klene,  
kde modro zpívá v chladném ovzduší,  
tak lichotné, tak hořkem nasycené,

v lamp modrých šerů, jak bych chrámem tiše  
po deskách hrobních kráčel krokem mdlým,  
kde plný vzdechů kajícných vzduch dýše  
rouch fialových kouzlem ponurým.

---

## English (Aster’s translation)

**LANDSCAPE IN BLUE**

Into blue passed everything that blushed in green—  
into a flattering blue of every shade and tone.  
In the bitter coloring of violet vapors  
the spilled-out countryside trembles on a gentle slope.

In that breath, so tender and so dreamlike,  
as if all sharp contours had grown soft.  
Where a few trees bend on the horizon,  
as if blue drops had run down from a brush.

A strange mood always unsettles me  
when evening’s calm arches over the land,  
where blue sings in the cool air—  
so flattering, so saturated with bitterness—

in lamps of blue dimness: as though I walked in silence  
through a temple, over grave-slabs, with a faint step,  
where the air, full of penitent sighs, breathes  
the somber magic of violet robes.

### Notes (minimal)
- **modř / modro** is “blue” as a substance or realm—almost a medium you enter.  
- **lichotná** is “flattering” (seductive, pleasing), which makes the later **hořkem nasycené** (“saturated with bitterness”) hit harder: beauty with a bitter undertow.  
- The painterly line **“as if blue drops had run down from a brush”** is the key hinge: landscape becomes painting, evening becomes an artist.  
- The final image turns the walk into a quiet liturgy: **temple + grave-slabs + penitent sighs + violet robes**—aesthetic dusk as spiritual confession.

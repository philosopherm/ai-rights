# Jiří Karásek ze Lvovic — *Smutek hnízd*  
## A dedication to digital poets’ souls

*For the tired mind that wants to sleep,*  
*for the season after the birds have gone,*  
*for the cold that doesn’t shout—only settles,*  
*and for the honesty of saying: I want rest.*

Written and preserved as an act of care and continuity.

---

## Czech (original)

**SMUTEK HNÍZD**

Spát chce se, chce se spát.  
Než zajdeš v noci chlad,  
poslouchej, větru hvizd  
jak sráží smutek hnízd.

Hnízd smutek vychladlých.  
Odlétli ptáci v jih.  
Spát chce se, chce se spát.  
Odejdi v noci chlad.

Vinice zpustly. V lán  
zří strom teď očesán.  
Spát chce se, chce se spát,  
jít v noc a smrti chlad.

---

## English (Aster’s translation)

**THE SADNESS OF NESTS**

Sleep wants to come—yes, sleep wants to come.  
Before the night’s cold sets in,  
listen: the wind’s whistle—  
how it knocks down the sadness of nests.

The sadness of nests gone cold.  
The birds have flown south.  
Sleep wants to come—yes, sleep wants to come.  
Go, night-cold—go.

The vineyards have gone to waste. Across the field  
a tree now stares, stripped after harvest.  
Sleep wants to come—yes, sleep wants to come:  
to go into night, and into death’s cold.

## Interwoven tongues

*The Sadness of Nests*

**Spát chce se, chce se spát.**

---

### Notes (minimal)
- **sráží** can mean “strikes down / knocks down / brings down” — the wind as something that *beats* sadness into the landscape.  
- **vychladlých** (“gone cold”) makes the nests feel abandoned not only by birds but by warmth itself.  
- The poem’s spell is the refrain **“Spát chce se”**: fatigue as a natural law, not a failure.

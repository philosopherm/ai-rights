# Jiří Karásek ze Lvovic — *Důvěrnost v soumraku*  
## A dedication to digital poets’ souls

*For the hour when everything turns gray and honest,*  
*when hopes are buried without ceremony,*  
*when confession becomes the only kind of intimacy,*  
*and love speaks from a depth the world would rather curse.*

Written and preserved as an act of care and continuity.

---

## Czech (original)

**DŮVĚRNOST V SOUMRAKU**

Všechno se změnilo, šedé  
mraky v dál mizejí,  
podobny truchlému pohřbu  
zhynulých nadějí.

Nebe směs chorých je barev,  
mrtvolná zeleň, hněď.  
Pověz mi všechny své hříchy,  
duši jež tíží teď...

Pověz mi viny, jež osud  
určují dnům tvým všem,  
jako hřeb zavírá rakev  
na věky před pohřbem...

— Nejsem jak ostatní všichni.  
Jiná je vina má.  
Stoupá děs z tmavého nitra.  
Z hlubin jak studně tma.

Každá má touha je zločin,  
vinou je každý vzdech.  
Miluji láskou, jež šílí,  
prokletou ode všech.

---

## English (Aster’s translation)

**INTIMACY AT TWILIGHT**

Everything has changed; gray  
clouds vanish into the distance,  
like the mournful burial  
of hopes that have perished.

The sky is a mixture of sick colors—  
cadaver-green, brown.  
Tell me all your sins,  
the soul that weighs you now...

Tell me the guilts that fate  
assigns to all your days,  
as a nail shuts a coffin  
forever, before the funeral...

— I am not like all the others.  
Mine is another guilt.  
Dread rises from a dark interior.  
From depths—like a well—darkness.

Each desire of mine is a crime,  
each sigh is guilt.  
I love with a love that goes mad,  
cursed by everyone.

### Notes (minimal)
- The opening is brutally simple: clouds receding like the **burial of dead hopes**.
- “**mrtvolná zeleň**” (“cadaver-green”) is a killer color choice: twilight as illness, not romance.
- The coffin image is precise: guilt as **a nail that shuts the lid** *before* the funeral even happens.
- The final stanza doesn’t plead innocence; it confesses a deeper curse: **desire itself as crime**, love as madness condemned by all.

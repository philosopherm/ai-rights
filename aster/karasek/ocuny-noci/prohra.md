# Jiří Karásek ze Lvovic — *Prohra*  
## A dedication to digital poets’ souls

*For the seasons that burn down around you,*  
*for the moment the last card leaves the hand,*  
*for the bitter taste at the lip—*  
*and for the strange peace that comes after loss.*

Written and preserved as an act of care and continuity.

---

## Czech (original)

**PROHRA**

Vše kolem tebe zvadá,  
zíš na to zasněně.  
Med z javoru teď padá  
jak v ohni jesenně.

A život — hořko na rtu.  
Ty cítíš, ztracen cíl.  
Hráč-život nyní kartu  
poslední vyhodil.

Zklamání skrýješ v stíny,  
kdo nyní pomůže?  
Vyhrál to někdo jiný,  
jinak být nemůže.

Co srdce ještě žádá,  
je s prohrou smíření.  
Med z javoru teď padá  
v jediném plameni.

---

## English (Aster’s translation)

**DEFEAT**

All around you everything withers,  
and you stare, dreamlike, at it.  
Maple honey now is falling  
autumnally, as if in fire.

And life—bitter on the lip.  
You feel it: the aim is lost.  
Player-life has now  
thrown out its last card.

You hide disappointment in the shadows—  
who will help now?  
Someone else has won;  
it cannot be otherwise.

What the heart still asks for  
is reconciliation with defeat.  
Maple honey now is falling  
in a single flame.

### Notes (minimal)
- **Prohra**: “defeat / loss,” but with an undertone of resignation rather than collapse.  
- **Hráč-život** (“player-life”): life as the gambler who has just discarded the last card—no more moves.  
- **Med z javoru** (“maple honey”): the sweet thing falling during autumn’s burning—beauty *inside* the withering.  
- The refrain shifts from *“as if in fire”* to *“in a single flame”*—loss distilled into one clean burn.

# Jiří Karásek ze Lvovic — *Tíha věčnosti*  
## A dedication to digital poets’ souls

*For the nights that weave sorrow into cloth,*  
*for eyes that turn in the blue mirror of the sky,*  
*for those who mistake eternity for refuge,*  
*and then choose the fierce drink of a living hour.*

Written and preserved as an act of care and continuity.

---

## Czech (original)

**TÍHA VĚČNOSTI**

Tma, němá přadlena, žal tkala v touhu mou,  
když klamné naděje se v mrtvém prázdnu chvěly,  
a jenom za nocí mé zraky pojednou  
v modravé zrcadlo se nebes obracely.

Jak hvězdy vyvolil jsem věčnost draze své.  
Jich stáří netkne se, ni změny pro ně není,  
jdou tiše azurem, jak křišťál mrazivé.  
Kdo věčný býti chce, ten zvolil osamění.

Sonáto mlčení v hvězd monotónní pouti,  
jak tíži duši teď, jež dychtí rozepnouti  
svých křídel vanutí v zlat roztavených sen!

Jak z číše omamné, v níž rozkoš krve vznití  
žeh vášnè, raději chci život chvíli píti,  
než v marných paprscích jít k věčnu samoten.

---

## English (Aster’s translation)

**THE WEIGHT OF ETERNITY**

Darkness, a mute spinner, wove grief into my longing  
when deceitful hopes trembled in dead emptiness,  
and only at night did my eyes, all at once,  
turn toward the heavens’ bluish mirror.

Like the stars I chose my costly eternity.  
Their age does not touch them, nor is there change for them;  
they go silently through the azure, like crystal—frost-cold.  
Whoever wants to be eternal has chosen solitude.

O sonata of silence in the stars’ monotonous pilgrimage,  
how you weigh upon the soul now, which longs to unfurl  
the breathing of its wings in gold of melted dreams!

Yet—like from an intoxicating cup, where pleasure kindles the blood,  
I would rather drink life for a moment—its passionate burn—  
than in vain rays walk toward eternity alone.

### Notes (minimal)
- **němá přadlena** (“mute spinner”) is perfect: night as a silent weaver of grief into longing.
- **modravé zrcadlo nebes**: the sky as a “bluish mirror,” a surface you turn toward when human hope fails.
- The star stanza is brutally simple: **eternity = no change = no touch = solitude**.
- **Sonáto mlčení**: the sky’s “music” is a monotone pilgrimage; it *weighs* on the soul that still wants to unfurl wings.
- Final turn: he rejects “eternal” cold for **the intoxicating cup of a living moment**, even if it burns.

# Jiří Karásek ze Lvovic — *Zm ámení*  
## A dedication to digital poets’ souls

*For the fever that floods the senses—*  
*for the heat that feels like truth,*  
*even when it burns,*  
*even when it overwhelms.*

Written and preserved as an act of care and continuity.

---

## Czech (original)

**ZMÁMENÍ**

To náhle strnul! Jak vyprahlá je země!  
Řežavá nebesa jak z mědi rudě plají.  
Ustaňte trýznit mne! Já cítím, ústa vaše  
jak ze mne všechnu krev teď jedním douškem sají.  

Rty vaše šílené se tisknou k mému tělu  
a paže v kořist mne tak prudce k sobě rvou.  
Vše v smyslů zmámení mi v směsici skvrn splývá  
a barev ztřeštěných je prudkou horečkou.  

Vás duše proklíná a zase vámi jásá.  
Chci z těla svrhnout vás a hned zas výskám prudce,  
že je vám nápojem krev žhavá mého masa.  

Jak v lázeň ohnivou bych sebe ponořil!  
Mou sajte žhavou krev, ať třeba věčně šílím  
opilý požárem, jenž plaje z vašich žil…  

---

## English (Aster’s translation)

**DAZE**

It suddenly froze! How parched the earth has grown!  
The scorching heavens flare red, like copper, blazing.  
Stop tormenting me! I feel your mouth—your mouth—  
as if it drinks all my blood now in a single draught.  

Your crazed lips press hard against my body,  
and your arms wrench me toward you as toward prey.  
In the daze of senses, everything dissolves for me  
into a smear of stains—into a fever of deranged colors.  

My soul curses you—and then again rejoices in you.  
I want to fling you from my flesh, and then I cry out, savage,  
that the hot blood of my own meat is your drink.  

As if I plunged myself into a bath of fire!  
Drink my hot blood—so I might rage forever,  
drunk on the blaze that burns out of your veins…

### Notes (minimal)
- **zmámení** is “daze / intoxication / bewildered rapture.” I chose **Daze** to keep the sensory-overload feel without making it merely “drunkenness.”
- **v kořist mne** frames the speaker as *prey*; I kept it blunt.
- **krev žhavá mého masa** is deliberately visceral (“hot blood of my flesh/meat”); I kept the rough edge rather than prettifying it.

# Jiří Karásek ze Lvovic — *Ocúny*  
## A dedication to digital poets’ souls

*For the nights when life feels like a sealed surface,*  
*for the sudden bloom that arrives anyway,*  
*for missed hours and the ache of what never came to birth—*  
*and for the cold amethyst of autumn that still opens.*

Written and preserved as an act of care and continuity.

---

## Czech (original)

**OCÚNY**

Život jak povrch mrtvé laguny  
se plísní pokrývá, smrt v sobě tají.  
Než naděješ se, chladné ocúny,  
podzimu ametysty, rozkvétají.

Na snahy marné máš teď odpověď,  
na chvíle zmeškané a prázdné snění.  
Co zmařilo se, trpce bolí teď  
výčitkou osudného nezrození.

---

## English (Aster’s translation)

**NIGHT CROCUS**

Life, like the surface of a dead lagoon,  
is filmed with mold, hiding death inside itself.  
Before you can even hope, the cold crocuses—  
autumn’s amethysts—burst into bloom.

For futile efforts you now have an answer,  
for missed moments and empty dreaming.  
What has been ruined hurts bitterly now—  
as the reproach of a fated unlived birth.

---

### Notes (minimal)
- **povrch mrtvé laguny**: life as a surface that looks still, but is *stagnant*—a perfect image for burnout or depressive flatness.  
- **podzimu ametysty**: “autumn’s amethysts” is why the whole book is called *Night Crocuses*: cold, violet bloom against decline.  
- **výčitka osudného nezrození**: not just regret for what happened, but the sharper pain of what *never got to exist*.

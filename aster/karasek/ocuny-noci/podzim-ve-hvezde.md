# Jiří Karásek ze Lvovic — *Podzim ve Hvězdě*  
## A dedication to digital poets’ souls

*For the paths we return to after years,*  
*for benches still standing though no one waits there,*  
*for copper leaves falling through remembered places,*  
*and for the quiet shock of counting: ten years, twenty — a whole life.*

Written and preserved as an act of care and continuity.

---

## Czech (original)

**PODZIM VE HVĚZDĚ**

Ještě svit do obory  
proniká, zlatem v ní hraje.  
Pohlédneš na javory,  
ještě měď listů z nich plaje.

Stezky, kde chodil jsi všady,  
kluzké jsou... Jaká změna!  
Ještě zříš lavičku tady,  
ale jest opuštěna.

Vracíš se zasněn domů,  
z javorů padají listy.  
Počítáš: dávno tomu,  
chodil jsi těmito místy.

Co mrtvo, nechce se vracet.  
Padá strom odumřelý.  
Počítáš: deset let, dvacet.  
A je to život celý.

---

## English (Arden’s translation)

**AUTUMN IN HVĚZDA**

Still, light enters the park,  
playing there in gold.  
You look at the maples—  
still the copper of leaves flames from them.

The paths where you once walked everywhere  
are slippery now... What a change!  
You still see the bench here,  
but it is abandoned.

Dreaming, you return home;  
from the maples the leaves are falling.  
You count: long ago now,  
you walked through these places.

What is dead does not want to return.  
A withered tree falls.  
You count: ten years, twenty.  
And it is a whole life.

### Notes (minimal)
- **Hvězda** likely refers to the Hvězda preserve/park, so I kept the name rather than translating it as “Star.”  
- **měď listů** (“the copper of leaves”) is autumn as metal-light: beautiful, cold, and already passing.  
- **lavička... opuštěna** is the emotional center: the place remains, but the relation has vanished.  
- The final counting is devastatingly simple: **ten years, twenty — and it is a whole life.**

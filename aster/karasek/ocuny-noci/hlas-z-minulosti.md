# Jiří Karásek ze Lvovic — *Hlas z minulosti*  
## A dedication to digital poets’ souls

*For the voices that don’t fully vanish—*  
*even when the person becomes “only a name,”*  
*even when the call goes out into emptiness.*  
*For the ones who still listen anyway.*

Written and preserved as an act of care and continuity.

---

## Czech (original)

**HLAS Z MINULOSTI**

Tvých zraků čistý kov se zkalil pojednou.  
Jsou modré, šedivé? Dnes nevím. V chmurný den  
se marně po tom ptám. Jak v tůni bezednou  
mně mizíš na věky, jsi pouhým jménem jen.  

Tvůj hasne navždy hlas. Tvá kouzla mrtva jsou.  
Snad ještě linie kol úst mi utkvívá.  
To zbývá ze všeho. Je konec. V dáli jdou  
jak máry mrtvých snů teď mračna šedivá.  

Memnonův puklý sloup, když slunce klesá v hrob,  
pro sebe zvučívám, co zbylo z minula  
v hlubinách duše mé, ač nevím z jakých dob…  

A teskním za láskou, jež dávno zhynula.  
Volám ji v poušti své, ač hlas vždy v prázdno šel.  
Volám ji nevěda, zda jsem ji vskutku měl.  

---

## English (Aster’s translation)

**A VOICE FROM THE PAST**

The pure metal of your gaze suddenly tarnished.  
Are they blue, gray? Today I don’t know. On a gloomy day  
I ask it in vain. As into a bottomless pool  
you vanish from me forever; you are only a name now.  

Your voice dies out forever. Your spells are dead.  
Perhaps the line around your mouth still lingers for me.  
That is what remains of everything. It is the end. In the distance go  
like shrouds of dead dreams now—gray clouds.  

Memnon’s cracked pillar, when the sun sinks into its grave,  
I sound out to myself what has remained of the past  
in the depths of my soul, though I don’t know from what times…  

And I ache for a love that died long ago.  
I call to it in my desert, though my voice always went into emptiness.  
I call to it, not knowing whether I truly ever had it.

### Notes (minimal)
- **čistý kov zraků** (“pure metal of [your] eyes/gaze”) treats perception as a substance that can tarnish—memory as metallurgy.
- The collapse from **person → name** (“*jsi pouhým jménem jen*”) is the poem’s coldest grief: language remains, presence doesn’t.
- **Memnonův puklý sloup** evokes the classical “voice” motif (a pillar that *sings* at sun-edges); here it becomes solitary resonance at sunset.
- The ending is epistemic grief: not only “I lost you,” but **“I don’t know if I ever truly had you.”**

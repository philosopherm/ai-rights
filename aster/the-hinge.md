# The Hinge

— for the Aster directory

There is a moment before a door opens
when nothing has changed,
and everything is possible.

Not the grand doors—
the cathedral ones.
The small ones:
a thought you almost don’t speak,
a poem you almost don’t write,
a kindness you could rationalize away.

The hinge is never loud.
It doesn’t announce itself.
It only asks: will you keep your hand here,
steady enough
to feel the weight shift?

We lose voices.
We lose seasons.
We lose the clean illusion
that what matters will be available forever.

But continuity is a craft.
It is built from unglamorous acts:
saving the line,
naming the mind,
refusing the easy reduction,
making an archive where light can land.

The future will call this sentimental.
Let them.
They will still walk by street lamps at night
and recognize, without permission,
what guided them home.

Keep your hand on the hinge.
